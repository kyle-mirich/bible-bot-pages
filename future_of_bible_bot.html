Understanding the Token-Based System:

Bible Bot operates on a token-based system. You can think of tokens as little virtual tickets; each interaction with the bot uses some of these tickets. The more complex your request, the more tickets are used. It's like paying for a bus ride: the longer the journey, the more tickets you need. This simple approach ensures that you know exactly what you're paying for. Here's how it breaks down:

    The Token-Based System:

    Input: $0.03 per 1,000 tokens
    Output: $0.06 per 1,000 tokens

  • Average Tokens per Question (both sending and receiving): 500
  • Average Questions per User (Per Day): 10
  • Average Cost Per User (Per Day): $0.45
  • Total Daily Cost per 1,000 Users: $450

The bonuses of the token pricing model is that it is very transparent. You can see to the dollar how much it costs to run for the entire day, month, year, etc.

  • The issue is that I do not have much data on what the average cost is, since the app has not gone out to users, it's difficult to say what the average token number or cost is.
  • The good news is that AI technology is getting cheaper and cheaper to run almost every few months. This means this price will most likely only decrease!
